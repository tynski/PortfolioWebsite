Bachelor thesis

The paper presents different approaches to the design and implementation of complete classification systems. The system attempts to unambiguously divide patients into healthy and sick based on multidimensional vectors of characteristics describing human voice. For this purpose, 1 374 recordings of extended vowels /a/, /i/, /u/ in normal tone were used. The data set was taken from the Saarbruecken Voice Database (SVD). A binary classification model was designed, which segregates patients into healthy and sick. Several versions of parameter vectors extracted from the audio signal in the time, frequency and cepstral domain were presented and tested. The final version of the proposed classification system is composed of a vector of 17 parameters. The research presents possible solutions together with their qualitative evaluation. The system has been implemented in Python language and scikit-learn library.
